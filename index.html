Biotech valuation defies standard DCF logic. Drugs fail, timelines stretch, and the science itself is often the primary value driver. Core Principle — Updated for 2025 At pre-clinical through Phase I, value lives in optionality and the team's ability to generate de-risking data. At Phase II, value migrates to probability-weighted peak sales forecasts. For TechBio/AI-native platforms, an independent technology floor exists regardless of individual drug outcomes. The 2024–25 market has shifted decisively toward clinical validation before significant capital deployment — price accordingly.
Primary Method
Risk-Adjusted NPV
Discount future cash flows by cumulative PTRS (probability of technical and regulatory success). Key: the discount rate reflects only systematic market risk — program-specific execution risk is handled through explicit phase-transition probabilities, not bundled into the rate. (Harvard/Analysis Group, 2024)
Calibration Tool
Comparable Transactions
Anchor to real deal values: M&A precedents, licensing fees, and public company EV/pipeline ratios stratified by modality and stage. OrbiMed uses peak sales estimates from Bloomberg and Visible Alpha alongside EV/pipeline ratios as a cross-check on private valuations.
Qualitative Layer
Venture Scorecard
Structured scoring on team, IP, market, platform vs. single-asset, and competitive moat. Translates into premium/discount on the rNPV output. Dominant method at pre-IND; weight declines as clinical data accumulates.
Platform Signal
Real Options + Platform Floor
For platform and TechBio companies, model optionality on follow-on indications, licensing rights, and data asset value. ARTIS Ventures principle: even if the drug fails FDA, there's still technology value on the table — the platform floor is a separate, additive valuation layer.
Protocol 00B

2025 Market Context

The 2020–21 biotech boom, followed by the 2022–23 correction, has produced a structurally different investment environment. Valuations and investor behavior have reset in ways that materially affect how you should enter and price deals today.

Macro Shift
Flight to Quality
The market has shifted decisively from the broad-based approach of 2020–21 to a more selective, thesis-driven strategy. Investors are writing larger checks for fewer companies, concentrating on proven teams, validated science, and clear market opportunity. Seed and early-stage investments face significant capital constraints as emerging managers struggle to form new funds.
Capital Flows
$26B Deployed in 2024
JPMorgan characterized 2024 as "strong" for biopharma VC: $26B invested across 416 rounds (up from $23.3B / 462 rounds in 2023). US healthcare startups raised $23B in 2024, with ~30% flowing to AI-driven firms. Mega-rounds ($100M+) are increasingly dominant — 7 biotech companies raised $300M+ in first-time financings in 2024.
Entry Point Shift
Later-Stage Bias
Top-tier investors including Novo Holdings and Sofinnova have shifted entry points later — preferring late preclinical or clinical-stage compounds with validated biology. Investors are reducing risk with a strong preference for animal validation or clinical data before follow-on investment. Pre-IND bets require exceptional teams and platforms to attract institutional capital.
New Category
TechBio Emergence
AI-native drug discovery platforms have become a distinct asset class attracting dedicated capital. 31 AI-discovered assets are in clinical phases as of 2025. The Recursion–Exscientia merger ($688M) validated that investors will pay for AI platforms and data assets independent of individual drug outcomes. Tech giants (Google, NVIDIA, Microsoft) are taking direct positions in biotech.
Exit Environment
M&A Remains Primary
M&A exits in 2024 totaled $82B — down from $178B in 2023 — but deal volume remained healthy. IPO markets have become highly selective; only the strongest Phase II readouts qualify. Strategic acquirers (Takeda, Novartis, AstraZeneca) are driving M&A to refresh pipelines expiring exclusivity on blockbusters. An estimated $200B in pharma revenues face patent cliffs 2024–2028.
IRA Impact
Pricing Risk is Real
The Inflation Reduction Act introduced Medicare price negotiation after certain post-approval windows. Small molecule drugs face earlier negotiation timelines than biologics (9 vs. 13 years), creating a structural bias toward biologics, cell/gene therapies, and rare disease assets. Adjust peak sales projections and revenue duration assumptions accordingly for any small molecule in common indications.

Protocol 01B

Science Gate — Pre-Model Biology Check

Adapted from RA Capital's evidence-based investing methodology: before building any financial model, the science must pass a structured biology gate. Garbage-in on scientific assumptions destroys any downstream valuation. This gate is binary — fail any hard stop, and rNPV work doesn't begin.

RA Capital Principle RA Capital's process starts with a single question: does the science make sense? The firm evaluates the underlying biology of every company's approach as the foundation of the entire investment thesis — not as a formality, but as the primary filter. Deep scientific knowledge paired with strategic capital is the investment model. Financial modeling comes second.
GateQuestionHard Stop CriteriaGreen Light Criteria
Target Validation Is there human genetic evidence the target is causal in disease? Animal-only, no human genetics link, no loss-of-function data GWAS support, Mendelian randomization, or clinical LoF/GoF evidence
Biological Rationale Is the mechanism of action fully elucidated and logical? "We believe it works through X" without direct mechanistic evidence Structural biology confirmed, on-target selectivity panel clean, MOA directly demonstrated
Data Reproducibility Have key results been independently replicated? Single-lab results, no replication, unexpected difficulty reproducing key data CRO confirmation, independent academic replication, or multi-site preclinical package
Translational Bridge Is there a credible path from animal to human? Rodent-only efficacy with no translatable biomarker or mechanistic bridge Multi-species data, validated pharmacodynamic biomarker, disease-relevant model
PTRS Sanity Check Does the scientific premise support the assumed probability of success? Team using top-decile PTRS assumptions for bottom-decile evidence quality PTRS assumptions benchmarked to modality and indication, stress-tested with external KOL
KOL Alignment Do leading experts in the field agree the science is sound? Founding team cannot name 2+ independent KOLs who support the approach SAB includes active field-defining KOLs who have reviewed the data and engaged seriously
ARCH Venture Insight ARCH's model is built on being willing to sit down with a scientist who has only a lab notebook. But the prerequisite is still fundamental scientific conviction — they back great science first, then build the company around it. For investors who are not company co-founders, the bar must be even higher: you need both great science AND an existing team capable of executing toward a clinical inflection point.
Protocol 02

Risk-Adjusted NPV (rNPV) Model

The rNPV adjusts each future cash flow (or milestone) by its probability of being realized, then discounts at a venture-appropriate rate. The formula: rNPV = Σ [CF_t × PoTS_t] / (1+r)^t − Development Costs. Build one model per asset; sum across the pipeline.

Protocol 05

Real Options & Platform Value

Platform companies — where the primary product is the technology engine, not a single drug — require an additional valuation layer. Real options theory captures the value of future choices that management can make depending on how early data unfolds.

Option Type
Option to Expand
Additional indications, geographies, or combination therapies enabled by a positive Phase II read. Value = PV of follow-on programs × probability of exercise. Key driver for platform companies.
Option Type
Option to Abandon
The ability to stop development if early signals are negative. Value is in preserving capital. Negative optionality that is often ignored but substantially reduces downside in preclinical bets.
Option Type
Option to Partner/License
Licensing upfront or milestone rights to a Big Pharma at Phase II. Sets a floor on program value. Particularly valuable in capital-intensive modalities (CGT, gene editing).
Option Type
Option to Defer
Ability to wait for competitive landscape clarity, pricing precedents, or manufacturing improvements before committing capital to Phase III. Especially relevant in crowded indications.
Platform Valuation Rule of Thumb Model the lead program via rNPV. Then add platform premium = (number of credible follow-on indications × 0.3) × lead program rNPV. Discount aggressively for execution risk. For very early platforms, the premium typically adds 50–200% over the single-asset value.
Protocol 05B

TechBio / AI-Native Platform Valuation

TechBio companies — where AI, machine learning, or computational biology is core to the value creation engine, not just a tool — require a fundamentally different valuation lens. Standard rNPV systematically undervalues these companies by treating the platform as invisible.

ARTIS Ventures Principle (coined "TechBio") "Even if the drug didn't make it through the FDA, there's still value on the table because of the technology — how to make the drug, or how to discover the drug. That's the key difference." The platform has independent value from any single drug outcome. This creates a valuation floor that pure rNPV misses entirely.
Value Layer 1
Drug Pipeline (rNPV)
Model individual programs using standard rNPV. Sum across pipeline. This is the floor — what the company is worth even if the platform generates zero additional value beyond current programs. Weight by PTRS and stage.
Value Layer 2
Platform Productivity Premium
How many new drug candidates can the platform generate per year? At what cost vs. traditional discovery? Model platform as a drug-generation machine: (new programs/year × avg rNPV per program × platform survival rate) discounted at a technology business rate (20–30%), not a drug development rate.
Value Layer 3
Data Asset Value
Proprietary biological datasets, experimental output from AI-driven labs, and validated model weights constitute defensible moats. Value as a pharma partnership asset: benchmark against deal upfronts for data/platform access deals (Novo Nordisk–Valo: $60M upfront, $2.7B in milestones). Data assets can be licensed independently of drug outcomes.
Value Layer 4
Tech Sector Multiple (Revenue)
If the company generates platform SaaS/licensing revenue (CRO/pharma partnerships), apply a hybrid tech-biotech multiple. AI-driven discovery platforms with recurring revenue can trade at 8–15× ARR — closer to a technology multiple than a biotech multiple. Key: revenue must be real and contract-backed, not "strategic interest."

TechBio Diligence Checklist
QuestionWhat to Look For
AI Claim Validity Can the team prove the AI is doing the work, not just branding? Show prospective validation, not retrospective fitting.
Data Ownership Is the proprietary data actually proprietary? Who owns it under pharma partnerships? CDA/IP terms matter enormously.
Compute Moat Is compute access a real barrier, or will it be commoditized? Durable moats are in data + model architecture, not GPU access alone.
Wet Lab Integration Does the company generate its own experimental data, or rely on public datasets? Self-generating platforms are more defensible.

Red Flag: AI Washing The most common TechBio failure mode: applying ML to biology without genuine predictive power. Ask for prospective validation experiments — where the model made a prediction, then the prediction was tested wet-lab. Retrospective fitting of historical data does not validate an AI platform. Require at least 2 prospective successes before assigning any platform premium.
Key Insight: 2024 Recursion–Exscientia Merger The $688M all-stock acquisition validated that investors pay for AI platforms and data assets independent of individual drug outcomes. Recursion paid for Exscientia's ML models and pharma partnership infrastructure — not its drug pipeline. This is the first major public proof point for the platform floor valuation concept.

Team Assessment

The #1 predictor of success In early-stage biotech, team quality explains more variance in outcomes than any scientific parameter. The company will pivot. The science will surprise. Only great teams navigate that successfully.

Team Risk Factors
CRITICAL RISK

Academic founder(s) with no plans to hire an operating CEO. Science-only team with no clinical development or regulatory experience. Founder conflicts or IP ownership disputes.

MEDIUM RISK

Thin management bench, over-reliance on one founder. No clinical advisor or KOL engaged in the primary indication. Misaligned incentive structures.

POSITIVE SIGNAL

Prior successful drug developer on founding team. SAB includes field-defining KOLs who are active, not just named. Industry co-founder or operator already in seat.

Protocol 08

Market & Competitive Analysis

Biotech market sizing is notoriously inflated. Apply incidence-based modeling rather than prevalence-based forecasts. Anchor to realistic penetration curves and treatment duration assumptions.

TAM Methodology
Incidence × Treatment Rate
New patients per year × diagnosis rate × treatment rate × % who reach target therapy × annual cost. Far more conservative and defensible than top-down estimates.
Competitive Moat
Differentiation Framework
First-in-class vs. best-in-class vs. fast-follower. Assess: efficacy delta, dosing convenience, safety profile, patient access. Needs a credible answer on why payers will reimburse.
Pricing Power
ICER & Reimbursement
Model an ICER (incremental cost-effectiveness ratio) for the indication. $150K/QALY is roughly the US payer threshold. Gene therapies and rare disease may support $500K–$3M+.
Competitive Timeline
Entrant Risk
Map the Phase I/II competitive pipeline (clinicaltrials.gov). Time to market for #1 competitor relative to your company. Assess risk of new entrant data readouts compressing your window.

Deal Structure Considerations
Milestone-Linked Tranches
For high-uncertainty bets, structure investment in tranches tied to de-risking milestones (IND clearance, Phase I safety readout, PoC data). Reduces binary risk; aligns founder incentives with capital deployment. In 2024–25 market, milestone structuring has become standard practice even for well-regarded teams at pre-IND stage.
Portfolio Construction
Conviction Sizing Model
At PoTS of 5–10%, a portfolio of 20+ companies is needed to expect at least 1 approval — if bets are equal-sized. The ARCH model suggests instead sizing by conviction: 3–5 high-conviction lead positions (5–10% of fund each) plus a broader portfolio of smaller positions for option value. Reserve 40–60% of fund for follow-on in breakout companies.
Exit Pathways
M&A Is the Base Case
Most biotech exits are M&A (pharma buyout at Phase II–III). An estimated $200B in pharma revenues face patent cliffs 2024–2028, keeping strategic acquirers active. IPO window is highly selective — only ~15% of private biotechs are estimated to meet current quality thresholds. Model base case at 5–7 years to M&A exit; structure early to be acquirable, not just fundable.
Return Expectations
Target Multiple by Stage
Pre-clinical: 10× minimum target; Phase I: 5–8×; Phase II PoC: 3–5×. TechBio platforms require separate return modeling: platform productivity value + drug rNPV. Adjust for modality risk (CGT and gene editing require higher multiples). IRA impact: reduce revenue duration assumption for small molecules in common indications by 2–3 years vs. pre-IRA models.

(3) Is our entry price supported by at least two independent methods, anchored to 2024–25 benchmarks (not 2021)? (4) Have we stress-tested PTRS assumptions with an external KOL who has no financial interest? (5) What are the two or three realistic exit pathways, and can the company reach them without requiring a market cycle we can't control? (6) Have we accounted for IRA pricing dynamics on the revenue projection? (7) Can we explain in one sentence why this company will still be relevant in 10 years?
